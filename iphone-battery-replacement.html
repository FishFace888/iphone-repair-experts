Does your iPhone need a new battery?

Signs your battery needs replacing

iPhone batteries degrade over time. Here are the most common signs that your battery is on its way out and a replacement will make a real difference.

📉
Battery drains fast
Your phone used to last all day. Now it barely makes it to lunchtime. Rapid battery drain is the most obvious sign of a degraded battery and it gets worse over time.
💀
Unexpected shutdowns
Your phone switches off at 20%, 30% or even 40% battery. This happens when the battery can no longer deliver consistent power and iOS shuts down to protect the device.
🐢
Phone feels slow
Apple intentionally slows down iPhones with degraded batteries to prevent unexpected shutdowns. If your phone has become noticeably sluggish, a new battery often fixes it completely.
🌡️
Phone runs hot
A battery working harder than it should generates more heat. If your iPhone gets warm during normal use or while charging, a failing battery is a likely cause.
📊
Battery health below 80%
Apple themselves recommend battery replacement when health drops below 80%. You can check yours in Settings. At 80% or below, performance and runtime are noticeably affected.
🔌
Won't charge properly
Battery stuck at a certain percentage, slow to charge, or showing inconsistent charge levels. These can all indicate a battery that has lost its ability to hold and accept a charge correctly.
Important safety information

Is your screen lifting at the edges?

This could be a swollen battery — and it needs attention promptly.

⚠️
A swollen battery is a safety issue, not just a performance one
As iPhone batteries age and degrade, they can swell with gas produced by internal chemical reactions. A swollen battery pushes outward against the screen and casing, which is why the screen may appear to be lifting or separating from the body of the phone. Left unaddressed, a swollen battery can damage the screen from the inside, cause the display to stop working, and in rare cases poses a risk of rupture. If your screen is lifting at the edges or the back of your phone appears bowed, stop using the device for charging and get in touch with us straight away. We can come to you the same day.

How to check your battery health

Your iPhone tracks battery health automatically. Here is how to find yours in under a minute.

Apple measures battery health as a percentage of original capacity. A brand new iPhone starts at 100%. The lower the number, the less charge your battery can hold compared to when it was new. Apple recommends replacement at 80% or below.

The hidden reason your phone feels slow

Apple slows your iPhone to protect a dying battery

This is something many iPhone users don't know. Apple introduced a feature called performance management that deliberately limits your iPhone's processor speed when the battery is no longer able to deliver consistent power. The reason is to prevent unexpected shutdowns, which happen when a degraded battery can't supply the burst of power needed for demanding tasks.

The effect is real and noticeable. Your phone may feel sluggish opening apps, slow switching between tasks, or laggy when scrolling. Many people assume their phone is just getting old and that it's time for an upgrade. In most cases it is not. The phone is fine. The battery needs replacing.

A new battery restores your iPhone's processor to full speed. For many customers this feels like getting a new phone, at a fraction of the cost.
