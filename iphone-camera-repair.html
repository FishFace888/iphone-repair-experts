Signs your iPhone camera needs repairing

Camera faults show up in a lot of different ways. Here are the most common problems our customers describe when they get in touch.

⬛
Black screen when you open the camera
You open the camera app and get a black screen instead of a live view. The app opens but nothing appears. This is one of the most common camera faults and in most cases means the camera module itself needs replacing.
🌫️
Blurry or foggy photos
Every photo you take comes out blurry, soft or foggy regardless of how still you hold the phone. If cleaning the lens makes no difference, the fault is internal. The autofocus mechanism or the camera module itself is likely damaged.
🔍
Camera won't focus
The camera struggles to lock onto a subject or spins continuously without settling. You can see the image shifting in and out of focus but it never lands. This is usually a fault with the optical image stabilisation or autofocus components inside the camera module.
💥
Cracked or smashed lens glass
The glass over your rear camera is cracked or shattered from a drop. Even if the camera still works, a cracked lens will get worse and eventually affect image quality. It also leaves the camera module underneath exposed to dust and moisture.
🤳
Front camera not working
The rear camera works but the front camera is dead, black, or showing a distorted image. Front camera faults are common after drops where the screen takes the impact. In some cases the front camera ribbon cable is the cause rather than the camera itself.
⚡
Flash not working
The flash does not fire when you take a photo or use the torch. The flash LED and the rear camera share the same module on most iPhone models, so a flash fault often indicates a wider camera module issue. We test and repair both at the same time.

Why cameras fail

What causes iPhone camera damage?

Understanding the cause can help you protect your camera after the repair.

💥
Drops and impact
The most common cause of camera failure. A drop does not need to crack the screen to damage the camera. The internal ribbon cables and connectors that link the camera to the motherboard are delicate and can be displaced or broken by the impact of a fall.
💧
Water and moisture
Despite water resistance ratings, moisture can still work its way into the camera over time, particularly around the lens seals on older models. This causes fogging on photos, corrosion on the camera contacts, or complete failure of the module.
🔧
Previous repair damage
A screen replacement carried out without care can disconnect or damage the front camera ribbon cable. If your camera stopped working after a screen repair elsewhere, this is almost certainly the cause and is a straightforward fix.
🌡️
Heat exposure
Leaving your phone on a dashboard in direct sunlight or in a hot environment can damage the optical image stabilisation mechanism inside the camera. The gyroscope and stabiliser components are sensitive to sustained high temperatures.
⏰
Age and wear
On older iPhones, the optical image stabilisation motor can wear out over time, causing blurry photos and focus problems. This is common on iPhone 6 Plus, 7 Plus and iPhone X models and is a standard repair rather than a write-off.
🪨
Cracked lens glass
A drop that cracks the lens glass over the camera does not always immediately affect image quality. But a cracked lens will spread, and once the glass fragments begin to interfere with the optics, photos will deteriorate quickly. Early repair prevents further damage.
