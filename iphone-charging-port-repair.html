Is this your problem?

Signs your charging port needs repairing

Charging port problems are one of the most common iPhone repairs we carry out. Here are the most frequent symptoms our customers describe.

🔌
iPhone won't charge at all
You plug in the cable and nothing happens. No charging animation, no response. The phone may appear completely dead even if the battery has charge remaining. This is the most urgent charging port symptom.
📐
Only charges at a certain angle
You have to hold the cable at a specific angle or prop the phone up to keep it charging. This is a classic sign of a worn or damaged dock connector that has lost a reliable connection with the cable.
🔁
Charges intermittently
The phone starts and stops charging on its own, or the charging percentage goes up and down unpredictably. Inconsistent charging is a strong indicator that the port is damaged or worn internally.
😮
Cable feels loose or falls out
The charging cable no longer clips in firmly and falls out with the slightest movement. The retention clips inside the dock connector have worn away, meaning the cable cannot maintain a proper connection.
💻
iPhone not recognised by computer
Your iPhone no longer shows up in iTunes or Finder when connected via cable. This indicates a dock connector fault affecting data transfer as well as charging — both functions share the same port.
🐢
Charges very slowly
The phone charges, but much slower than it used to. What once took an hour now takes several. A partially damaged dock connector can still pass some current but not enough to charge at normal speed.
Don't assume the worst

Your iPhone looks completely dead. It might just be the port.

📱
A dead-looking iPhone is not necessarily a dead iPhone
Many customers contact us convinced their iPhone has died completely — blank screen, no response, nothing. In a significant number of cases, the culprit is a faulty charging port. If the phone has not been able to charge for long enough, the battery will fully drain and the phone will appear completely unresponsive. Once a new dock connector is fitted and the phone takes a charge, it powers back on. If your iPhone has gone completely dead, a charging port repair is absolutely worth trying before writing the phone off. Get in touch and we will advise honestly on whether it is worth attempting.

Know your connector

Lightning port or USB-C port?

iPhone 14 and below use a Lightning connector. iPhone 15 and above use USB-C. We repair both. Here is how to tell which one your iPhone has.

⚡ Lightning port

iPhone 5, 6, 7, 8, SE, X, XR, XS, XS Max, 11, 12, 13, 14 and all Plus and Pro models up to iPhone 14 Pro Max
•Oval shaped connector, narrower than USB-C
•Apple's proprietary connector used from 2012 to 2023
•Compatible with Lightning cables and MagSafe chargers
•We carry replacement Lightning dock connectors for all models

🔌 USB-C port

iPhone 15, 15 Plus, 15 Pro, 15 Pro Max, iPhone 16, 16 Plus, 16 Pro, 16 Pro Max
•Rounded rectangular connector, wider than Lightning
•Industry standard connector introduced on iPhone 15 in 2023
•Compatible with USB-C cables and chargers
•We carry replacement USB-C dock connectors for all current models

What causes charging port damage

Why do charging ports fail?

Understanding what causes the problem can help prevent it happening again after your repair.

🔄
Everyday wear and tear
Every time you plug and unplug your charger, the dock connector experiences mechanical stress. Over thousands of insertions the internal pins and retention clips wear down, eventually causing a poor or failed connection.
💧
Moisture and water damage
Even small amounts of moisture entering the port can cause corrosion on the internal contacts over time. This is particularly common in phones used outdoors or carried in pockets during exercise.
⚡
Cheap or damaged cables
Low quality or damaged cables can cause excess friction and stress on the dock connector. Cables that are kinked or frayed near the connector end are particularly damaging to the port over time.
💥
Physical damage
Dropping the phone while a cable is connected, or catching the cable on something, can bend or break the internal pins inside the port. Even a single incident can cause immediate charging failure.
🌡️
Heat and temperature extremes
Leaving your phone in a hot car or using it in extreme cold can affect the port over time. Temperature cycling causes the materials inside the connector to expand and contract, which can weaken connections.
⏰
Age
Charging ports simply wear out over time. If your iPhone is three or more years old and charging is becoming unreliable, the dock connector has likely reached the end of its useful life and a replacement will restore full function.
